P1: Test a Perceptual Phenomenon¶

Statistics: The Science of Decisions Project Instructions

Background Information¶

In a Stroop task, participants are presented with a list of words, with each word displayed in a color of ink. The participants task is to say out loud the color of the ink in which the word is printed. The task has two conditions: a congruent words condition, and an incongruent words condition. In the congruent words condition, the words being displayed are color words whose names match the colors in which they are printed: for example RED, BLUE. In the incongruent words condition, the words displayed are color words whose names do not match the colors in which they are printed: for example PURPLE, ORANGE. In each case, we measure the time it takes to name the ink colors in equally-sized lists. Each participant will go through and record a time from each condition.

1. What is our independent variable? What is our dependent variable?¶

Independent variable is the type of Stroop task being performed (congruent or incongruent). Dependent variable is the time it took participant to perform each part of the Stroop tasks.

2. What is an appropriate set of hypotheses for this task? What kind of statistical test do you expect to perform? Justify your choices.¶

We wish to test if the participants of the Stroop task take longer to identify the names of the colors when the colors are the same (congruent) compared to when the colors are different (incongruent). To accomplish this, we record the time it takes for participants to perform both Stroop tasks (identifying names of colors when colors and names are congruent and incongruent).

H_0: Population mean time to complete incongruent task = Population mean time to complete congruent task

H_1: Population mean time to complete incongruent task > Population mean time to complete congruent task

Our null hypothesis is that there is no significant difference between the population mean times of incongruent tasks and congruent tasks. Our alternative hypothesis is that there is a significant increase in population mean time to perform incongruent task versus congruent task. We use a one-tailed paired t-test to test this hypothesis since all participants performed both tasks, we are only interested in seeing if there is an increase (one tailed), sample sizes are small (less than 30), and standard deviation is not known.

# calculating critical value
df_diff = dataFrame['Incongruent'] - dataFrame['Congruent']
mean_diff = df_diff.mean()
std_diff = df_diff.std()
n = df_diff.count()
df = n - 1
alpha = 0.01
t_critical = scipy.stats.t.ppf(1-alpha,df)

print "mean difference is:", mean_diff
print "standard deviation of difference is:", std_diff
print "critical value for t-test with", alpha,"level of confidence and", df, "degrees of freedom:", t_critical

mean difference is: 7.96479166667
standard deviation of difference is: 4.86482691036
critical value for t-test with 0.01 level of confidence and 23 degrees of freedom: 2.49986673949

For a one sided 1% confidence level, the critical value is about 2.5. The t-statistic from our test gives a value of 8.02. Since our t-statistic is greater than the critical value, we reject the null hypothesis at 1% confidence level.

We can then say that we observe significantly increased times to complete incongruent tasks versus congruent tasks.
